Fayne issues completion certificates automatically. When a student finishes every lesson in a course, the platform generates a certificate and makes it available to them immediately — no action required from you.

## How certificates work

Certificates are triggered by course completion. A course is considered complete when a student has progressed through all published lessons in it. At that point:

1. Fayne generates a certificate branded with your academy's name.
2. The certificate becomes available to the student in their learning dashboard.
3. The student can download or share it.

You do not need to configure anything, approve anything, or manually issue certificates. The process is fully automated.

<Info>
Certificates display your academy name, the course title, the student's name, and the completion date. The branding reflects your academy identity — students see your name, not Fayne's.
</Info>

## What triggers completion

A course is marked complete when the student has finished all published lessons in that course. Unpublished lessons do not count toward or against the completion threshold.

If you add new lessons to a course after a student has already completed it, their completion status is not retroactively changed. Students who complete the expanded course after the new lessons are published will receive a fresh certificate.

<Note>
If you need to reset a student's progress for a course — for example, so they can retake it — you can do that from the student detail panel under the **Enrollments** tab. Resetting progress will also clear their certificate for that course.
</Note>
