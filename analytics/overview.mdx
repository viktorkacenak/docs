The **Analytics** section of your owner dashboard gives you a complete picture of how your academy is performing — from how many students have enrolled in each course to how much revenue you have collected this month. Analytics are scoped to the academy you are currently viewing, so if you run multiple academies, use the academy switcher in the top bar to move between their respective data.

## Course-level analytics

The course analytics view shows you how each course in your academy is performing across three key dimensions.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Enrollment count" icon="users">
    The total number of students who have enrolled in the course, including students who are still in progress and those who have completed it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Completion rate" icon="circle-check">
    The percentage of enrolled students who have completed every lesson in the course. A low completion rate can indicate that a course is too long, poorly structured, or not matching student expectations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Engagement" icon="chart-bar">
    A measure of active learning activity in the course — lesson views, time spent, and progress events. High enrollment with low engagement often signals that students are signing up but not starting.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Sort your courses by completion rate to find your strongest and weakest performers. Courses with high enrollment but low completion are the best candidates for content review or structural improvements.
</Tip>

## Student-level analytics

Click any student in the **Students** page to open their detail view, which includes analytics specific to that individual.

- **Lesson views:** the number of times the student has opened each lesson, giving you a sense of which lessons they are revisiting.
- **Completions:** which lessons and courses the student has marked as complete.
- **Time spent:** an estimate of total time the student has spent in the course viewer.

<Info>
  Student-level time spent is an estimate based on session activity. It is useful for spotting broad patterns — for example, a student who has spent significant time on a lesson but not marked it complete — but it is not a precise attendance record.
</Info>

## Activity audit log

The audit log records every significant action taken by anyone with owner or team access to your academy. Each entry includes who performed the action, what they did, and when.

Actions captured in the audit log include:

- Creating, editing, or deleting a lesson or course
- Publishing or unpublishing a course
- Inviting or removing a team member
- Changing branding or settings
- Connecting or disconnecting Stripe

<Note>
  The audit log is read-only. It is designed for accountability and review, not for undoing changes. If you need to reverse a content edit, use the version history in the lesson editor.
</Note>

## Revenue analytics

The revenue section of your dashboard gives you an at-a-glance view of your academy's financial performance. For detailed transaction history, dispute management, and payout schedules, follow the **Open Stripe dashboard** link at the top of the revenue section.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Total revenue" icon="circle-dollar-sign">
    Cumulative revenue collected across all paid enrollments and active subscriptions since your academy launched.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Monthly trend" icon="chart-line">
    A month-by-month chart showing revenue collected over time. Use this to track growth and identify seasonal patterns.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Per-course breakdown" icon="book-open">
    Revenue attributed to each individual course, so you can see which courses are driving the most sales and where to focus promotion.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stripe dashboard link" icon="arrow-up-right">
    A direct link to your full Stripe dashboard for complete transaction records, individual customer details, and payout history.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Revenue figures in your Fayne dashboard reflect completed payments processed through Stripe. Refunded or disputed transactions are not automatically deducted from the displayed totals. Check your Stripe dashboard for net revenue after refunds.
</Warning>
