Every lesson in your academy can be edited using plain English instructions. Instead of manually rewriting paragraphs, you type what you want in the AI chat bar at the bottom of the editor and Fayne applies the changes for you. The AI reads the lesson you are working on, the course it belongs to, and the context you have saved for your academy before making any edits. That context means the changes it makes fit naturally into what is already there.

## How to use the AI editor

Open any lesson in your course editor. The AI chat input appears as a floating bar at the bottom of the screen. Type your instruction and press **Enter**. The AI reads your request and the current lesson, then applies the changes directly to your lesson as a working draft. You can keep sending instructions, undo a step, or discard your unpublished changes at any time before you publish.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lesson editor">
    Navigate to **Courses**, select a course, and click any lesson in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type your instruction">
    Click the AI chat bar at the bottom of the screen and describe what you want. No special syntax required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="See the changes applied">
    The AI updates the affected blocks in your lesson and the editor reloads to show the result. Read through what changed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep editing or publish">
    Send more instructions to refine, undo a step, or switch to manual editing. Your changes stay as a draft until you publish, so nothing reaches students until you are ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  AI changes are applied to your draft, not published automatically. Students see updates only after you publish the lesson. You can always discard unpublished changes and restore your last published version from the editor.
</Note>

## Block-level AI operations

The AI targets specific blocks in the lesson rather than rewriting the whole document at once. The underlying operations are:

| Operation | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Replace** | Rewrites the content of an existing block in place. Used for both rewriting and expanding text. |
| **Insert** | Adds a new block before or after an existing one, or at the top or end of the lesson. |
| **Delete** | Removes a block from the lesson entirely. |

Changes are written straight into your draft. If you do not like a result, send another instruction, undo the step, or discard your unpublished changes.

## Context the AI uses

The AI does not edit blindly. Before generating any response, it reads:

- The full content of the lesson you are editing
- The course the lesson belongs to
- The context you have saved for your academy: the brand voice, audience, and examples you provide

This means when you ask for "a B2B example," the AI draws on the context you have given your academy rather than producing a generic one. When you ask it to "add a quiz," it writes questions relevant to the lesson topic, not placeholder text.

<Note>
  AI edits preserve your formatting. Output stays clean: no rogue HTML, no inline styles, no extra markup. Every change uses the same block structure as the rest of your lesson.
</Note>

## AI editing and your content

Lessons you add from the course library are copied into your academy as your own private versions (see [Course Library](/content/course-library)). When you edit a lesson, whether by hand or with AI, you are editing that copy.

- Your edits never affect the master library or any other academy.
- The master library never overwrites your copy.
- Until you publish, AI changes live in your draft, and you can discard them to restore your last published version.
