The course library is Fayne's catalog of professionally written business courses, browsed from the **Explore** area of your dashboard. Every course is organized into a Track, a themed collection of related courses covering a domain like Sales, Marketing, Leadership, or Operations. You start here when setting up your academy, and you can return at any time to add more courses as your audience grows. The core promise of the library is that the content is already done: you browse, preview, and add, rather than write from scratch.

## How courses are organized

The library uses a four-level hierarchy:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Lesson" icon="file-text">
    The atomic unit. Each lesson covers one focused topic and takes 10 to 20 minutes to complete.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Course" icon="book-open">
    A sequence of 5 to 15 lessons with a clear progression. For example: "Cold Outreach Mastery."
  </Card>

  <Card title="Track" icon="layers">
    A collection of 3 to 7 related courses on one domain. For example: "Sales Mastery Track" groups cold outreach, discovery calls, and closing courses together.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Academy" icon="graduation-cap">
    Your complete branded product. It is what students see as one unified destination at your subdomain.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Browsing the library

Navigate to **Explore** from your academy dashboard. Courses are grouped by Track. For each course you can see:

- **Lesson count**: how many lessons are in the course
- **Estimated duration**: total time to complete the course
- **Full outline**: all lesson titles in order
- **Preview**: open a read-only view of the lesson content before adding

Use the Track view to see how multiple courses fit together as a curriculum. If a Track aligns with your audience, add the courses you want from it.

## Adding courses to your academy

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse or search">
    Open **Explore** and browse by Track, or use the search bar to find a specific topic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview before adding">
    Click any course to see the full outline. Click any lesson title to preview its content before committing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a course">
    Click **Add to academy** on any course. The course appears in your course list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Preview a few lessons in a course before adding it. This helps you confirm the tone and depth match your audience's expectations before you customize or publish.
</Tip>

## Building a custom course

You are not limited to the pre-made courses in the library. You can create your own course from scratch and write its lessons yourself, by hand or with AI.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new course">
    Go to **Courses** and click **Create new course**. Give it a name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add lessons">
    Open the course and click **Add lesson** to add a new, blank lesson, then write it in the block editor. Draft lessons by hand or use AI to help, and repeat to build out the course.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Arrange the order">
    Drag and drop lessons in the course outline into the order you want. The course structure is yours to define.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set pricing and publish">
    Set a price (free or a one-time price) and publish the course.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The Explore Library and your copies

When you add a course from the Explore Library, Fayne copies each lesson into your academy as your own private version. You own those copies and edit them in place.

- **Adding a course copies its lessons into your academy.** You can edit any lesson, by hand or with AI, without affecting the Explore Library.
- **Your copies are independent.** Edits in your academy never change the Explore Library or any other academy, and the Explore Library never overwrites your copies.
- **Explore Library improvements apply to future additions.** When the content team improves a course, those updates reach academies that add the course afterward. Courses already in your academy stay as they are.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Your copies" icon="git-branch">

  </Card>

  <Card title="Explore Library" icon="book">

<Note>
  The lesson editor lets you discard unpublished changes and restore your last published version. There is no link back to the Explore Library once a course has been added, so there is no "reset to original" step.
</Note>
